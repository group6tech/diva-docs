Jobs

To display a list of jobs, on DIVA Fusion's menu, Server, then click Jobs.

Here you will find jobs that:

  • are queued and waiting to run
  • Status = Queued
  • are currently running
  • Status = Running
  • have been completed
  • Status = Completed
  • have failed because of an error, and
  • Status = Error
  • have been cancelled
  • Status = Cancelled.

Filter the List of Jobs

If the list of jobs is too long and you are finding it difficult to locate a particular job or set of jobs, you can shorten the list using the filter options below.

Filter List of Jobs

Once you have located the jobs you are looking for, you can:

  • select one or more failed jobs (jobs with an error) and/or queued jobs, then click Cancel Selected to cancel the jobs.
  • select one or more failed jobs (jobs with an error) and/or cancelled jobs, then click Retry Selected to run them.

Purging Completed and Cancelled Jobs

Completed and cancelled jobs are automatically purged (removed) from the list of jobs after seven days. You can, however, purge them at any time.

Note: You can set the period for which completed and cancelled jobs are retained; after which these job records will be automatically purged. To do this, on the Fusion menu, click System, click Settings, and under Jobs, overtype the Retention Period value.

Before You Begin

Before purging all completed and cancelled jobs, ensure the list of jobs is displayed. To do this, on the Fusion menu, click Server, then click Jobs.

To purge completed and cancelled jobs:

  1. On the Jobs Queue page, click Purge Completed.
    • Result: You are asked if you want to purge all completed and cancelled jobs.
  2. Click Purge Jobs.
    • Result: All completed and cancelled jobs are removed from the list.

Cancelling a Job

Jobs that have failed, or jobs that are queued to run can be cancelled.

Cancel Queued Jobs

Before You Begin

Before cancelling a queued or failed job, ensure the list of jobs is displayed. To do this, on the Fusion menu, click Server, then click Jobs.

To cancel a job:

  1. Select the relevant job.
    • Result: Once a queued or failed job has been selected, The 'Cancel Selected' button is enabled.
    • Note: You can select more than one job to be cancelled. Only selected queued and failed jobs will be cancelled.
  2. Click Cancel Selected.
    • Result: The job is cancelled.

What's Next?

Once a job has been cancelled, you can re-run it or purge it from the list.

Note: Jobs that have failed because of an error may fail again. When purging, all completed and cancelled job records are removed from the list. This cannot be undone.

  • Back to top

Queuing a Failed or Cancelled Job

Failed and cancelled jobs can be put back in the queue. A job that has failed previously may fail again.

Before You Begin

Before putting a failed or cancelled job back in the queue, ensure the list of jobs is displayed. To do this, on the Fusion menu, click Server, then click Jobs.

To queue a failed or cancelled job:

  1. Select the relevant job.
    • Result: Once a failed or cancelled job has been selected, The 'Cancel Selected' button is enabled.
    • Note: You can select more than one job to be put in the queue. Only the failed and cancelled jobs that have been selected will be put in the queue.
  2. Click Retry Selected.
    • Result: The failed and cancelled jobs that you selected are placed in the queue.

Event Levels

Event Levels

Events fall into one of three levels:

  • Information
  • Warning
  • Error

Information

If there is an information level event, this means that something of note has occurred. Users can still use their drives.

Warnings

If there is a warning, this means that there is a problem that may affect users' ability to access their drives.

The following is a list of some of the warnings that may be displayed:

  • Directory cannot be accessed
  • Directory is corrupt
  • Directory in use
  • Directory not found
  • Directory path is not a legal form
  • Directory path is too long
  • File cannot be accessed
  • Job has been running for more than 5 minutes
  • Unknown node is trying to connect
  • Volume has decreased in capacity
  • Volume has increased in capacity.

Errors

If there is an error, this means that users cannot access or use some or all of their drives.

The following is a list of some of the errors that may be displayed:

  • Directory is not empty
  • Drive is missing on a node
  • MXF folder for a user is missing
  • Job has failed to complete
  • Job has timed out
  • Directory junction cannot be deleted
  • License has been exceeded
  • License key is missing
  • Node is not on the expected IP
  • Node IP cannot be deleted
  • Node IP is offline
  • Volume is offline

Stopping Event Notifications

The event log tracks events that can disrupt users' productivity. Depending on how notifications are set up, an event will trigger a series of emails. For an event, emails will be sent over a 24 hour period unless resolved or acknowledged.

In addition to an email being sent as soon as it is detected, further notifications of active events are emailed:

  • half an hour after the event started
  • one hour after the event started
  • six hours after the event started, and
  • 24 hours after the event started.

Note: Once an event is resolved, or no longer recurring, there is a five minute delay before the event record is closed.

Before You Begin

Before stopping event notifications, ensure the event log is displayed. To do this, on the Fusion menu, click System, then click Event Log.

To stop event notifications:

  1. On the Event Log page, review the events.
    • Note: Where relevant, respond to and resolve the events.
  2. Click Acknowledge All Incidents.
    • Result: For all current events, email notifications will cease.
    • Note: Future events will be unaffected and will initiate emailed notifications until resolved or acknowledged.

Note: If the same event recurs within 28 days, it is set to 'Active' and until acknowledged or resolved, it will generate a new set of emailed notifications.

Setting the Notifications You Receive

You can decide whether you want to receive:

  • Information, Warning and Error Notifications
  • To receive notifications for all three notification level events, select Information.
  • Warning and Error Notifications
  • To receive notifications for warning and error level events, select Warning. You will not receive notification of any information level events.
  • Error Notifications
  • To receive notifications for error events only, select Error. You will not receive notification of any information, nor warning level events.

Repeating Notifications Option

If this option is selected, you will be emailed a notification as soon as the event is detected. Further notifications of active events will be emailed:

  • half an hour after the event started
  • one hour after the event started
  • six hours after the event started, and
  • 24 hours after the event started.

If this option is not selected, you will be emailed a notification as soon as the event is detected. You will not receive further notification of the event.

Before You Begin

Before setting the notifications you receive, ensure the Settings page is displayed. To do this, on the Fusion menu, click your name, then click Settings.

To set the notifications you want to receive:

  1. From the Notification drop down menu, select the relevant option.
    • To receive notifications for all three notification level events, select Information.
    • To receive notifications for warning and error level events, select Warning.
    • To receive notifications for error level events only, select Error.
  2. Select or deselect the Repeating Notifications option.
    • Note: Select this option to receive notification of an event as soon as it is detected and subsequent notifications at the intervals stated above.
    • Note: Deselect this option to receive notification of an event as soon as it is detected. If the event remains active, you will not receive subsequent notifications about the event.
  3. Click Save Settings.
    • Result: Your changes to the settings are saved.
  • Back to top

Setting the Notifications Someone Else Receives

You can decide whether another administrator receives:

  • Information, Warning and Error Notifications
  • To receive notifications for all three notification level events, select Information.
  • Warning and Error Notifications, or
  • To receive notifications for warning and error level events, select Warning. The administrator will not receive notification of any information level events.
  • Error Notifications
  • To receive notifications for error events only, select Error. The administrator will not receive notification of any information, nor warning level events.

Repeating Notifications Option

If this option is selected, the administrator will be emailed a notification as soon as the event is detected. Further notifications of active events will be emailed:

  • half an hour after the event started
  • one hour after the event started
  • six hours after the event started, and
  • 24 hours after the event started.

If this option is not selected, the administrator will be emailed a notification as soon as the event is detected. The administrator will not receive further notification of the event.

Before You Begin

Before setting the notifications you receive, ensure the administrator's Properties page is displayed. To do this, from the Users menu, click Users, then click the administrator's user profile.

If the Notification Level drop down menu and Repeating Notifications option are not displayed, the user account is not an administrator's user account. These options are only available to Fusion administrators; not DIVA Client users.

To set the notifications you want the administrator to receive:

  1. From the Notification drop down menu, select the relevant option.
    • To receive notifications for all three notification level events, select Information.
    • To receive notifications for warning and error level events, select Warning.
    • To receive notifications for error level events only, select Error.
  2. Select or deselect the Repeating Notifications option.
    • Note: Select this option to receive notification of an event as soon as it is detected and subsequent notifications at the intervals stated above.
    • Note: Deselect this option to receive notification of an event as soon as it is detected. If the event remains active, you will not receive subsequent notifications about the event.
  3. Click Save User.
    • Result: Your changes to the administrator's settings are saved.

Disabling or Enabling Event Monitoring of a Volume

Removing an unrelated drive (non-DIVA drive) can trigger an event and notification. With DIVA Fusion, you can stop event records and emailed notifications that relate to non-DIVA drives.

Important: The monitoring of a drive-related event and subsequent notification, is linked to the Windows drive letter of the drive or volume. If the drive letter is subsequently mapped to another drive, it is the other drive that will be affected. If a drive letter of a non-DIVA drive is mapped to a DIVA volume, the monitoring of the drive letter, if disabled, will need to be enabled.

Before You Begin

Before disabling or enabling emailed notifications relating a drive, ensure you are logged into DIVA Fusion.

To disable or enable notifications relating to a drive:

  1. On the Fusion menu, click Server, then click Nodes.
    • Result: A list of DIVA nodes is displayed.
  2. Click the relevant node.
    • Note: Click the node to which the drive is assigned.
    • Result: The node's properties are displayed.
  3. Click Volumes.
    • Result: The volume's are displayed. Each volume has a small spanner or wrench icon.
  4. Click the relevant volume's wrench.
    • Result: A pie chart is displayed showing the amount of space (used and free space), along with a Monitor checkbox
  5. Select/Deselect the Monitor option.
    • Note: Select this option to enable emailed notifications.
    • Note: Select this option to disable emailed notifications.
  6. Click Save Changes.
    • Result: The changes to the monitoring are saved.
