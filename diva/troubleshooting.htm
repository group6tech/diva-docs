Troubleshooting


Problem Possible Scenario Solution
I can't sign in. Your username or password may have been entered incorrectly. Check and correct your username and password.
Note: Your password is case sensitive.
Your user account has been disabled. Contact your administrator to enable your user account.
A sign in attempt on the same computer has just failed. Wait one minute and try again.
You are signing into a different DIVA application. If your are a DIVA Client user, ensure you are signing into DIVA Client.
If you are an administrator, sign into DIVA Fusion.
For more information, see:
  • DIVA Client > Overview > Signing In & Out of DIVA Client
  • DIVA Fusion > Overview > Signing In & Out of DIVA Fusion
You're signed into DIVA Client on another computer. Sign out of DIVA Client on the other computer.
DIVA Server is offline. Ensure the server is powered on and connected to the network. Ensure the DIVA Server and Fusion applications are running.
I've performed an action in Fusion and it hasn't happened. A different job in the queue has failed and is holding up processing on the node Check the job queue for failed jobs and examine the errors.
I've mounted a drive on my Mac and it doesn't appear on my desktop. Finder is not set to display connected servers. Under Finder/Preferences ensure Connected Servers is selected.
I've shared a project and other users can't access it. The job to share the project has failed. Check the job queue for the failed project share job and view the reported errors.
I can't mount a drive because the mount button is disabled. The drive is in maintenance mode. Wait for the drive health check to complete.
The drive is missing. See 'A drive is missing' below.
The node is offline. Ensure the node is powered on and connected to the network. Ensure the DIVA Node application is running.
A drive is missing. The volume on which the drive resides is offline. Check the RAID status.
The drive folder on the node has been renamed. Rename the drive folder to the correct name and perform a health check on the drive.
The drive folder has been deleted from the node. Restore the drive from backup and perform a health check on the drive.
